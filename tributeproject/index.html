A.P.J Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Misile Man Of India


A.P.J. Abdul Kalam (born October 15, 1931, Rameswaram, India—died July 27, 2015, Shillong) was an Indian scientist and politician who played a leading role in the development of India’s missile and nuclear weapons programs. He was president of India from 2002 to 2007. His scientific achievements and popularity gained him the epithets “Missile Man” and “People’s President.” Early life and career Kalam was born in a town in Tamil Nadu state to a fishing boat owner from a once wealthy family. The youngest of five siblings, Kalam persevered with his education despite his impoverished circumstances. He earned a degree in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology and in 1958 joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO). In 1969 he moved to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where he was project director of the SLV-III, the first satellite launch vehicle that was both designed and produced in India. In 1980 SLV-III successfully released a satellite called Rohini into near-Earth orbit, taking India’s space program to the international stage. Kalam oversaw further development of launch vehicle technologies at ISRO, including the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle.

BIOGRAPHY

  • ISRO Contributions: Played a pivotal role in India’s civilian space program, especially in the development of the SLV-III, which launched the Rohini satellite.
  • Defense Achievements: Known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work on ballistic missile and launch vehicle technologies (e.g., Agni and Prithvi missiles).
  • Nuclear Program: Led the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, establishing India as a nuclear power.
  • 11th President of India: Served from 2002 to 2007; known as the "People’s President" for his accessibility and focus on youth and education.
  • Advocated for Vision 2020, aiming for India’s transformation into a developed nation.

      • Honors and Awards

    • Bharat Ratna (1997): India’s highest civilian award.
    • Padma Bhushan (1981) and Padma Vibhushan (1990).
    • Numerous honorary doctorates from global institutions.
